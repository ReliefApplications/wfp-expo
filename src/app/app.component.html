“People by Jerry” 2018 On 7 February 2018 a magnitude 7,5 earthquake affected over half a million people in Papua New Guinea. When organizing food distributions in the Southern Highlands, WFP asked the local councellors in Pau’a for lists of people who should receive assistance. Jerry immediately took what paper and pen he had available and walked through the area counting people and drawing circles. The next day he handed WFP colleagues multiple sheets of paper covered with circles on both sides. WFP distributed high energy biscuits, rice and canned tuna to 1140 people in Pau’a. Other lists - not Jerry’s - were used for the distributions. These 5 linocuts are freely inspired copies of Jerry’s work that Mairi Sun showed me on her return from Papua New Guinea after she took part in WFP’s response in the southern Highlands. They are a metaphor for the challenge of identifying, counting and representing people in need of assistance.
